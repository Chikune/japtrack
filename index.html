Help & guide

How Japtrack works, what each section does, and the logic behind the numbers.

The big idea

Japtrack is a private, local-first money tracker. Everything lives on this device — nothing is uploaded anywhere. You feed it transactions (by importing bank statements or adding them manually), and it turns them into spending breakdowns, budgets, forecasts and reports.

Two kinds of numbers matter here, and they work differently — this trips people up, so it's worth understanding up front:

  • Flow (money moving) — your transactions. Income, spending, transfers. Tracked automatically from what you import.
  • Balances / net worth (money you have) — manual snapshots you enter yourself. Adding transactions does not change your net worth — see Accounts & Net worth for why.

Dashboard

Your at-a-glance overview for the selected month: income, spending, net, savings rate, a trend chart, your top spending categories, upcoming bills, and account balances. It's read-only — a summary of everything else. Use the month arrows in the top bar to move between months.

Transactions

Every income, expense and transfer. Add them manually with + Add transaction, or bulk-import a bank statement under Imports & Rules. You can filter, search, select multiple rows, and bulk edit or delete.

Imports & Rules: upload a CSV/statement and Japtrack groups it by merchant and suggests categories. Merchant rules are remembered so the next time it sees "TESCO" it auto-fills Groceries. You control which rows become rules.

Refunds & reimbursements

When a refund is paired with its original charge during import, both sides are filed under a hidden "Refunds" category and excluded from spending and income — because nothing really left your account, it cancels out. This keeps your "actually spent" figure honest.

Money tagged Reimbursement (e.g. a work expense paid back) also reduces your real spending total, rather than showing as extra income.

Accounts & Net worth

This is the part that surprises people. Net worth is built from manual snapshots you enter, not from your transactions.

Why? Your transactions only cover the accounts you import. They don't know your investment growth, your pension, your crypto, the interest on savings, or accounts you haven't connected. If net worth were auto-calculated from transactions alone, it would be wrong. So instead you periodically take a snapshot — "here's what each account is actually worth today" — and Japtrack charts those over time.

⚠️
Adding a transfer doesn't change your net worth. Moving £200 from Current Account to Savings records the movement, but you're no richer or poorer — so your net worth only updates when you add a new snapshot.
🚧
On the roadmap: letting transactions optionally nudge account balances between snapshots. The tricky part is avoiding double-counting — a snapshot already reflects reality, so later transactions would need careful reconciliation. For now, snapshots are the single source of truth for balances.

Budgets

Set a monthly spending limit per category. The page shows what you've spent vs. budgeted, what's left, and your pace through the month. Budgets are compared against your actual transactions, so keep categories tidy for accurate tracking.

Bills & Subscriptions

Recurring payments (rent, Netflix, phone). Japtrack shows what's due this month, what's been paid, and your next due date. Some recurring items are detected from imports; you can also add them manually. If an imported charge matches an existing bill at a different price, Japtrack asks whether to update it or add a new one — so price changes don't create duplicates.

Goals

Savings targets. Track a goal manually, or pin it to one of your net-worth buckets so it updates as your snapshots grow.

Debt calculator

List your debts (balance, APR, minimum payment) and Japtrack plans the fastest route to debt-free using the Avalanche (highest interest first — saves the most) or Snowball (smallest balance first — quick wins) method. The projection chart shows your balance falling to zero; hover any point for that month's payment, interest, and remaining balance.

Forecast

Projects your account balance forward using recurring income/bills plus any one-off items you add. Use it to spot a tight month before it happens.

Reports

Deeper analysis: category trends over time, biggest movers, top merchants, and where your money actually goes. Driven by the same transaction data as everywhere else.

Data & privacy

Everything is stored locally on this device — never uploaded. You can export or back up your data from Settings → Import & Export. Bank-statement imports are processed on your machine; nothing leaves it.
